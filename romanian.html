Lesson 0 — Breaking the Barrier

The Psychological Warm-Up

Before a single grammar rule — we address the real reason you haven't learned a language yet. It's not intelligence. It's fear.

Ask Yourself Honestly

What specifically has stopped me from learning a language before?
Am I afraid of sounding foolish? Of being judged?
Do I believe I'm "bad at languages" or "too old to learn"?
Have I been overwhelmed by grammar rules and textbooks?

If you answered yes — you are normal. By Lesson 4, Petrov's students were speaking. By Lesson 16, they were having real conversations.

The 3 Liberating Truths

1
You Already Speak a Language
Your brain already has the machinery. We're just installing new software.
2
Romanian Is Easy for English Speakers
It's Romance (like French, Spanish). Thousands of cognates: informație, situație, televiziune. It's phonetic — no silent letters.
3
Mistakes Are the Method
Wrong Romanian is infinitely better than no Romanian. Every mistake is a micro-lesson.

Your Contract With Yourself

"I will speak Romanian badly, bravely, and often — starting today." Write it down.

Why 16 Lessons Work

Petrov creates a fireproof foundation — a structural skeleton so strong that even if you stop for months, it never fully disappears.

Â vs Î

Â = inside a word (România). Î = start/end of a word (în). They sound exactly the same.

The Most Important Lesson

The Verb Matrix — Petrov's "Multiplication Table"

3 Tenses × 3 Forms = 9 sentence types. Master this and you can say almost anything.

Don't Panic

Start with just the "eu" (I) row. Once natural, add "tu" and "el/ea." Focus on speed, not perfection.

The 3 Key Patterns

?
Question
Invert or use rising intonation. Vorbesc eu?
+
Affirmative
Subject + verb. Eu vorbesc.
−
Negative
Add NU before the verb. Eu nu vorbesc.

The 3 Tenses You Need

Future (colloquial) = o să + subjunctive. Present = conjugated verb. Past = am + participle. That's 90% of daily speech.

Formal future uses voi + infinitive: Voi vorbi. — for news, books, formal settings.

Word Order — Flexible!

Word Order Changes Emphasis

Eu merg la școală. — I go to school. (neutral)
La școală merg eu. — It's me who goes. (emphasis)
Merg la școală. — (I) go to school. (subject dropped)

Romanian often drops the subject pronoun because the verb ending tells you who's speaking.

Contracted Forms — Real Speech

How Romanians Really Talk

nu am → n-am · nu ai → n-ai · nu are → n-are
este → e · nu o să → n-o să
N-am timp. — I don't have time.
S-a terminat. — It's over.

Drill — Build Sentences From the Matrix

Pick any verb. Run it through all 9 cells. 20–30 seconds per verb. Do this 5× daily.

Combining Numbers

21 = douăzeci și unu · 35 = treizeci și cinci
47 = patruzeci și șapte · 99 = nouăzeci și nouă
200 = două sute · 500 = cinci sute · 2000 = două mii

The "DE" Rule — Numbers 20+

After numbers 20 and above, add de before the noun:

doi lei — 2 lei ✅ (no "de")
douăzeci de lei — 20 lei ✅ (with "de"!)
o sută de oameni — 100 people ✅

1–19 = no "de." 20+ = always "de."

Telling Time

Lesson 12 — Imperfect Tense

"I was doing / I used to do"

Ongoing or habitual past actions: "I used to…", "I was …ing". The imperfect is regular — even irregular verbs behave predictably here.

Imperfect Conjugation

Pronouna fia aveaa mergea vorbia lucra

noieramaveammergeamvorbeamlucram

ei/eleerauaveaumergeauvorbeaulucrau

Imperfect Pattern

Take the infinitive stem → add -am, -ai, -a, -am, -ați, -au. Works for almost every verb!

Imperfect vs. Perfect Compus

✅ Perfect — completed action
Am mâncat la ora 12.
I ate at 12. (done)
⏳ Imperfect — ongoing / habitual
Mâncam când a sunat.
I was eating when the phone rang.

Using the Imperfect

Când eram mic, locuiam la sat.
When I was small, I lived in the village.
Mergeam la școală în fiecare zi.
I used to go to school every day.
Afară ploua și era frig.
Outside it was raining and it was cold.
Ce făceai ieri la ora cinci?
What were you doing yesterday at 5?

Lesson 14 — The Subjunctive "SĂ"

Modal + SĂ + Verb

Romanian uses the subjunctive where English uses infinitives: "I want to go" = "Vreau să merg." Only 3rd person changes from present tense.

When to Use SĂ

After: vreau (I want), trebuie (must), pot (I can), o să (will), hai (let's), sper (I hope), cred (I think).

If… Then… (Dacă)

3 Types of "If" Sentences

Real (likely):
Dacă am timp, vin la tine. — If I have time, I'll come.
Dacă plouă, stau acasă. — If it rains, I stay home.

Unreal (hypothetical):
Dacă aș avea bani, aș călători. — If I had money, I would travel.
Dacă aș fi tu, aș pleca. — If I were you, I would leave.

Past unreal:
Dacă aș fi știut, aș fi venit. — If I had known, I would have come.
